Chapter 1: Introduction

Chapter goals:

  1. Gain a basic understanding of psychiatric genetics and genome-wide association studies.

  2. Understand the recent history of psychiatric genetics.

  3. Gain insight into the current state of psychiatric disorder research.

1.3 History

In the videos below, Dr. Kendler gives brief overview of some of the prominent players in psychiatry and psychiatric genetics, starting from the late 1700s through the mid-1900s. Part 1 highlights the work of Robert Burton, Prosper Lucas, Jenny Koller, Ernst Rüdin, and Eugen Bleuler, and discusses the darker periods of the history of psychiatric genetics during the late 1920’s and 1930’s.

Part 2 is a deeper dive into a single episode in the history of Psychiatry: “The Beginnings of the Debate Between the Mendelians and the Biometricians in Psychiatric Genetics”, focusing on four individuals: David Heron, Karl Pearson, Aaron Rosanoff, and Charles Davenport during 1913 to 1914.

Title: History of Psychiatric Genetics (Part 1)

Presenter(s): Ken Kendler

Length: 21:44

Link to video transcript here.

Title: History of Psychiatric Genetics (Part 2)
